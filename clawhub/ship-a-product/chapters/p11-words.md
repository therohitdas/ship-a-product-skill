# Words

## When to Load This

Load this when requirements, docs, onboarding copy, help text, or product voice are in question.

## What This Part Covers

- Chapter 59: There's Nothing Functional about a Functional Spec
- Chapter 60: Don't Do Dead Documents
- Chapter 61: Tell Me a Quick Story
- Chapter 62: Use Real Words
- Chapter 63: Personify Your Product

## Core Idea

Words are not an afterthought. They shape the product, reduce confusion, and often reveal whether the team actually understands what it is building.

## Chapter Routing

- **Ch 59** -> Functional specs are pretending to be certainty.
- **Ch 60** -> Documentation is going stale faster than the product.
- **Ch 61** -> Use short narrative scenarios to clarify behavior.
- **Ch 62** -> Replace jargon with language users already understand.
- **Ch 63** -> Give the product a coherent human tone.

## Practical Reads

- If onboarding is weak, the problem may be language before it is feature set.
- Story beats often communicate intent better than abstract documentation.
- This file pairs well with Interface Design because product copy is UI, not garnish.
