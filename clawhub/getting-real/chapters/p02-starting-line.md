# The Starting Line

## When to Load This

Load this when the problem is product framing: too much scope, weak problem definition,
unclear conviction, dependence on outside funding, or a project that already feels like a chore.

## What This Part Covers

- Chapter 4: Build Less
- Chapter 5: What's Your Problem?
- Chapter 6: Fund Yourself
- Chapter 7: Fix Time and Budget, Flex Scope
- Chapter 8: Have an Enemy
- Chapter 9: It Shouldn't be a Chore

## Core Idea

Start from restraint and sharp intent. The product should solve a real problem, fit real constraints,
stand for something, and be worth the emotional effort to build.

## Chapter Routing

- **Ch 4 Build Less** -> Scope is exploding before the first useful version exists.
- **Ch 5 What's Your Problem?** -> The team can describe features but not the pain being solved.
- **Ch 6 Fund Yourself** -> Advice on independence, control, and not bloating the company to satisfy financing.
- **Ch 7 Fix Time and Budget, Flex Scope** -> Deadlines are real but scope is being treated as sacred.
- **Ch 8 Have an Enemy** -> The product needs contrast, a point of view, or a clear rejection of incumbent norms.
- **Ch 9 It Shouldn't be a Chore** -> The project feels joyless, forced, or strategically dead.

## Practical Reads

- Use this part when the right move is subtraction, not acceleration.
- If the team says "we need more resources," this part often says "no, you need less ambition per release."
- If the product cannot clearly oppose a worse alternative, its value proposition is probably mush.
