# Process

## When to Load This

Load this when execution has slowed down, planning has outrun shipping,
or the team needs a more practical build loop.

## What This Part Covers

- Chapter 29: Race to Running Software
- Chapter 30: Rinse and Repeat
- Chapter 31: From Idea to Implementation
- Chapter 32: Avoid Preferences
- Chapter 33: "Done!"
- Chapter 34: Test in the Wild
- Chapter 35: Shrink Your Time

## Core Idea

The process should push you toward real software quickly, then cycle through feedback and improvement.
Short loops beat elegant planning.

## Chapter Routing

- **Ch 29 Race to Running Software** -> Too much upstream planning.
- **Ch 30 Rinse and Repeat** -> Need an iteration mindset instead of one-shot perfection.
- **Ch 31 From Idea to Implementation** -> The team needs a concrete path from concept to shipped behavior.
- **Ch 32 Avoid Preferences** -> Endless subjective debate is blocking forward motion.
- **Ch 33 "Done!"** -> Clarify what done means and stop dragging work forever.
- **Ch 34 Test in the Wild** -> Need real-world validation, not sterile internal confidence.
- **Ch 35 Shrink Your Time** -> Projects are too big; cut them into smaller windows.

## Practical Reads

- The book prefers shipping something testable over preserving certainty.
- Real usage is a better judge than committee preference.
- If the cycle time is long, almost everything gets worse.
