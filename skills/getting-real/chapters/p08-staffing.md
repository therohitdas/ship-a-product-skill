# Staffing

## When to Load This

Load this when hiring decisions, role expectations, or team composition are the issue.

## What This Part Covers

- Chapter 40: Hire Less and Hire Later
- Chapter 41: Kick the Tires
- Chapter 42: Actions, Not Words
- Chapter 43: Get Well Rounded Individuals
- Chapter 44: You Can't Fake Enthusiasm
- Chapter 45: Wordsmiths

## Core Idea

Hiring is expensive in every sense, so delay it, inspect for real ability, and prefer people who can think broadly and communicate clearly.

## Chapter Routing

- **Ch 40 Hire Less and Hire Later** -> Team wants to hire before simplifying the work.
- **Ch 41 Kick the Tires** -> Validate real skill before commitment.
- **Ch 42 Actions, Not Words** -> Favor demonstrated output over polished self-description.
- **Ch 43 Get Well Rounded Individuals** -> Value breadth and judgment, not narrow checkbox fits.
- **Ch 44 You Can't Fake Enthusiasm** -> Energy and care matter more than rehearsed answers.
- **Ch 45 Wordsmiths** -> Writing is not optional in product work.

## Practical Reads

- A bad hire creates drag far beyond salary cost.
- The book strongly prefers evidence of doing over interviews about doing.
- Good product people usually write better than average because clarity travels.
