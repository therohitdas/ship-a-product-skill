# Stay Lean

## When to Load This

Load this when the product, team, or codebase is getting heavy and change is becoming expensive.

## What This Part Covers

- Chapter 10: Less Mass
- Chapter 11: Lower Your Cost of Change
- Chapter 12: The Three Musketeers
- Chapter 13: Embrace Constraints
- Chapter 14: Be Yourself

## Core Idea

Smallness is not a temporary phase. It is a strategic advantage that keeps the product nimble,
the team decisive, and change affordable.

## Chapter Routing

- **Ch 10 Less Mass** -> Too many people, too many moving parts, too much ceremony.
- **Ch 11 Lower Your Cost of Change** -> Future changes feel scary because architecture or process is rigid.
- **Ch 12 The Three Musketeers** -> Designer, programmer, and copywriter need to work as a tight unit.
- **Ch 13 Embrace Constraints** -> A limitation is being treated like a curse instead of a design force.
- **Ch 14 Be Yourself** -> The product voice is imitative instead of distinct.

## Practical Reads

- Use this when someone wants to add process "just in case."
- The book's bias is clear: fewer people and fewer layers usually mean better work.
- Constraints are not excuses. They are editing tools.
