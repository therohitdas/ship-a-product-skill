# Glossary

**Build Less** - The default posture of the book: reduce scope until only the important product remains.
**Cost of Change** - How painful it is to revise the product later; Getting Real tries to keep this low.
**Epicenter** - The core of the product, the thing everything else depends on.
**Flex Scope** - Hold time and budget steady, then cut or reshape scope to fit reality.
**Getting Real** - Building the actual product instead of spending too much energy on representations of it.
**Hollywood Launch** - A launch with energy, narrative, and intentional attention.
**Human Solutions** - Problems solved with people or process instead of more software.
**Inline Upsell** - Showing upgrade value inside the product where context is strongest.
**Opinionated Software** - Software with clear defaults and strong choices instead of endless customization.
**Post-Launch Tuneup** - The cleanup and refinement period immediately after shipping.
**Real Words** - Clear language normal users understand, instead of internal jargon.
**Start With No** - Reject features first, then make them earn their way in.
**Stay Lean** - Keep the team, product, and codebase small enough to change quickly.
**Test in the Wild** - Put real software in front of real users rather than over-indexing on artificial lab conditions.
**Three State Solution** - Design for regular use, empty states, and error states up front.
