# Priorities

## When to Load This

Load this when the team cannot decide what matters most, keeps polishing details too early,
or is worrying about scale before the core value is obvious.

## What This Part Covers

- Chapter 15: What's the Big Idea?
- Chapter 16: Ignore Details Early On
- Chapter 17: It's a Problem When It's a Problem
- Chapter 18: Hire the Right Customers
- Chapter 19: Scale Later
- Chapter 20: Make Opinionated Software

## Core Idea

Figure out the central promise of the product, delay secondary concerns, and build for the right people
with strong defaults instead of timid flexibility.

## Chapter Routing

- **Ch 15 What's the Big Idea?** -> The product thesis is weak or forgettable.
- **Ch 16 Ignore Details Early On** -> The team is polishing corners before the core flow works.
- **Ch 17 It's a Problem When It's a Problem** -> Premature worry is stealing attention from real issues.
- **Ch 18 Hire the Right Customers** -> You need to choose the kind of user the product is for.
- **Ch 19 Scale Later** -> Infra or growth panic is arriving before traction or product truth.
- **Ch 20 Make Opinionated Software** -> There are too many settings because the team is scared to decide.

## Practical Reads

- If the team says "we serve everyone," load this part. That's usually bullshit.
- A strong opinion reduces both product complexity and marketing confusion.
- This part is good for founders who keep mistaking future hypotheticals for current blockers.
