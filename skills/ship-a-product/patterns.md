# Patterns

## Scope By Constraint
**When to use**: The roadmap is too big for the available time, money, or attention.
**How**: Lock time and budget, then cut scope until the product becomes shippable.
**Trade-offs**: You may disappoint people who want breadth, but you preserve momentum.

## No-First Feature Triage
**When to use**: New feature ideas keep piling up.
**How**: Start from "no," ask what core problem it solves, then only continue if the value is obvious.
**Trade-offs**: You will miss some edge-case requests, but you protect clarity.

## Interface-First Design
**When to use**: The team is arguing abstractly about what to build.
**How**: Sketch the real screens first, then back into logic and implementation.
**Trade-offs**: You may revise the backend plan later, but the user experience gets anchored early.

## Human Before Software
**When to use**: A workflow edge case seems to demand a complicated feature.
**How**: Ask whether support, policy, or manual handling solves it more cheaply.
**Trade-offs**: Less automation, but often better speed and less product clutter.

## Launch Then Learn
**When to use**: The product is close enough to useful, but fear is delaying release.
**How**: Ship the smallest honest version, observe behavior, then iterate fast.
**Trade-offs**: Imperfections are visible earlier, but reality arrives faster.

## Small-Team Throughput
**When to use**: Coordination overhead is slowing delivery.
**How**: Keep teams tiny, reduce meetings, and give people blocks of uninterrupted time.
**Trade-offs**: Requires trust and sharper hiring.

## Product Voice As UX
**When to use**: The interface is technically correct but still feels confusing or cold.
**How**: Rewrite labels, prompts, help text, and empty states as part of design.
**Trade-offs**: Demands good writing, not just good layout.

## Public Improvement Loop
**When to use**: You have launched and need growth without bloated planning.
**How**: Support users quickly, write publicly, fix meaningful bugs, and keep educating.
**Trade-offs**: Requires consistency more than theatrical bursts.
